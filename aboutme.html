Bio

I am a student researcher and tutor at Kent State University where I am currently studying Computer Science and Applied Mathematics.

My research experience spans the Artificial Intelligence Lab and the Big Data Intelligence Lab, with a focus on machine learning, graph neural networks, and transformer-based models. I have contributed as a co-author on peer-reviewed articles and as a contributor to book chapters, and I have worked on projects involving dynamic graph modeling, wavelet-based spectral methods, transformer architectures, and large-scale benchmark evaluation. My broader interests include scalable machine learning systems, recursive and iterative transformer architectures, energy-efficient AI, and the theoretical foundations of learning on structured and sequential data.

I began tutoring with the Academic Success Center in Fall 2025 and received an engagement award for the highest number of tutoring minutes for Math courses. I currently provide course support for Algebra for Calculus, Trigonometry, Calculus I & II, Applied Linear Algebra, Discrete Mathematics, and Decision Making Under Uncertainty, as well as Object-Oriented Programming, Data Structures and Algorithms, Design Patterns, Operating Systems, and Computer Architecture.

Additionally, I am a member of the Ohio Lambda Chapter of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity, where I currently serve as Scholarship Chair and Standards of Excellence Chair
